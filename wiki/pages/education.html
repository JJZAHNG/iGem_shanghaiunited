Overview

Allan Bloom said that “Education is the movement from darkness to light” .We hope to light up the pathway to Synthetic Biology for children in our community and all over the country. We aimed to use the social media platforms to bring videos for students who aren’t able toaccess lab work, and lead them to Synthetic Biology lab. We hope to leave no one behind. We also engage with museum and other organization to collaborate and work for popularization of science. The new generation need to know the global issues and work to improve them. Base on these ideas and reason, we design our educational events and popular science videos.

Primary Students

On June 23 2024, we attended the SymBiosis in Shanghai Pinghe high school. SymBiosis is a exhibition run by high schools students in Shanghai, and attracted many parents bring their children from 7-14 to visit. We aimed to introduce the concept of desertification, the current challenge of re-plantation, and possible solutions to this problem. Our main goal was to make people understand the importance of controlling desertification to protect our earth. We wanted to expand the knowledge about gene editing method and geographic influence of desertification to the middle school and primary school student.

Introduce Desertification

We focused on teaching and showing students the effects of desertification because it's important for them to understand the serious consequences if we don't work to protect our Earth. We have included some small facts about desertification, such as how much green land turns into desert each year and how much land are covered by desert. We also made a small model contains plant and soils as an example to demonstrate the effect of desertification better. During the activity, we found that most student and their parents didn’t really understand the concept of desertification. Most of them think desertification is just land turning into desert simply, but it’s not. For this reason, people showed a high curiosity while we are explaining the concept.

Gene Editing Methods

Apart from presenting desertification, we also present some area relate to molecular biology. For example, we teach them about the meaning of ZFN and TALEN. To make primary school student understand the concept better, we added some picture with fun emoji, so they can understand the meaning better. We also include some bacteria sample in culture dish in order to make student patriciate on the topic better. After we presented how to use culture dish to cultivate bacteria, some middle school student with basic biology knowledge showed great interest at molecular biology; they also start to gain some high degree on molecular biology .

Bacterial Cellulose and Future

We also introduced our ongoing iGEM project about a type of bacteria and cellulose. This cellulose is very water-retaining and can be used on plants to improve their rate of surviving in the desert. It's super helpful in fighting desertification, but it's also expensive. Our goal is to improve its productivity by changing part of its DNA, which could significantly reduce its cost. And we hope that more people can join us to help us achieve this goal. All for our planet, for a better tomorrow.

High School

School Talk in High School about SynBio and Environmental Issues

Our school talk was in June before final exams, we chose to start the talk in Shanghai Hongwen School as most members in our team are from this school which made us easier to meet each other offline.

Before the Talk

We designed a bulletin board and hung it at the school. The purpose of the board is to introduce iGEM which includes the competition itself, the history of iGEM, and the team members. It also consists of the basic information and principles of genetic engineering.

Talk about SynBio and Environmental Issues

On 14th June 2024, we gave a public speech in Chinese at the school theater begun at 2 o’clock in the afternoon. We invited students and teachers from the Hongwen High School department and introduced the concepts of synthetic biology and climate crisis to the high school students.

Environmental Issues

During the presentation, two members exposed the current situation of desertification and challenges of re-plantation. The combined effects of natural factors and human activities have destroyed land resources, leading to a decrease in available land for agriculture and animal husbandry, a decline in breeding capacity, a decrease in livestock carrying capacity, and a decrease in crop unit area. We mentioned some specific things they should do to protect the environment such as no wasting water and damaging plants on purpose.

SynBio and Research Methods

After explaining the background and purpose of our topic, our team introduced the research sequence of our topic as an example of synthetic biology. We explained the production of bacterial cellulose and introduced that by genetically engineering the bacteria to increase their cellulose production, and potentially increase the efficiency in re-plantation

Impact and Public Engagement

At the end of the speech, our team noticed some other social platforms such as Bilibili and wechat of the team for others who want to learn more about environmental issues and SynBio.

Desertification may seem far away from us, but the truth is that it not only directly affects people's daily lives but also the whole planet. Through this lecture, we hope everyone in the school will pay more attention to environmental protection and natural ecological research in the future. We wanted to evoke the curiosity and interest in SynBio to students who are not very familiar with this subject. Moreover, we hope everyone will support our team and our project.

iGEM teams

The Conference of China iGEMer Community (CCiC) is a national exchange event initiated independently by iGEM participating teams in China. It aims to provide a resource-sharing platform and promote mutual learning and exchange among iGEM teams and young synthetic biology enthusiasts in China. Since its first held in 2014, the CCiC Conference has played an important role in maintaining the positive and good development of the community as a continuous focus of the CCiC Executive Committee, a national exchange summit related to synthetic biology competitions in China, and an important academic communication platform for young synthetic biology enthusiasts in China. It has received good feedback from all industry, academia, and research sectors. At present, the CCiC Conference has become one of the largest academic conferences for college students in China.

Our CCIC Our presentation is in three parts. In the first section, we will give some data and background information about the problem we will solve by using a new modified bacteria: soil desertification. In the second section, we will introduce bacterial cellulose with its properties. In the last section, we will introduce the production of bacterial cellulose and outline the factors that affect the production of bacterial cellulose. Then finally give out the specific gene we need to edit. We talked about the definition of desertification: As global temperatures rise and the human population expands, more of the planet is vulnerable to desertification, which causes land degradation and loss of productivity. The factors that cause soil desertification can be arranged into two categories, human factors and natural factors. Urbanization, mining, farming, and ranching are the main human factors causing soil desertification. Drought and the type of soil are the natural factors. These will lead to soil erosion and an inability of land to retain water or to regrow plants. So our goal is to solve this problem and figure out how to prevent water and how to enhance the vegetation survival rate.

For how to enhance the vegetation survival rate, we decided to use a Bacterial Cellulose (BC) called Komagataeibacter xylinus DSM 6513. This BC is a smart biomaterial, an extracellular biopolymer synthesized by several bacteria belonging to the genera Acetobacter, Komagataeibacter, Agrobacterium, Bacillus, and Clostridium Botulin. The nanofibrous network constituting the BC structure consists of well-aligned three-dimensional nanofibers forming a high surface area and porous hydrogel layer. There are many advantages of bacterial cellulose. The reason that BC can prevent water loss is that the OH group and methyl in nanocellulose can form hydrogen bonds with water molecules, and absorb water molecules. Water molecules are fixed on the surface of nanocellulose, so a membrane is formed, which can help to prevent water loss.  First, BC can be obtained purely from the culture medium and actively excreted by the cells without any purification process. Secondly, BC naturally has high purity, high crystallinity, high water storage capacity, high degree of polymerization, high surface area, and chemical stability. This means that BC is capable of increasing water storage at the root part of the plant, as it’s eco-friendly and sustainable. Lastly, compared with other chemical high water storage capacity materials, such as Hydrogel, PAM, and CMC. They are usually expensive and can be broken up into some chemicals which cause leakage and pollution

The production of BC is been produced by two substances, fructose, and glucose. As we can see in this picture, some glucose and been catalyzed to glucose acid which reduces the production of Glucose, which leads to the limited production of bacterial cellulose. On another path, some of the fructose joins the TCA cycle and works for the energy required by the bacteria, this also limits the amount of Fructose that can be catalyzed to glucose 1-P and leads to a small amount of production in bacterial cellulose. However, we could not stop the path to the TCA cycle, otherwise, the cell would die because of the lack of energy. Therefore, our plan for gene editing is to overexpress the PGI gene and knock out the GDH gene.

This picture illustrates how to knock out and overexpressing the gene. We are going to design the carrier with the modified gene and then use electro-transformation to add the carrier into the plasma. Use the PGI gene as an example, the blue part represents hundreds of base pair before the PGI gene, which help us to find the exact position to overexpress. Then the blue crosses over with the blue, and red crosses over with red, therefore leading the black overexpressing gene to the PGI gene. In the example of the GDH gene. A double crossover took place, the blue gene crossed over with blue, red crossed over with red, and the black gene alone, which has been knocked out. We are also going to add resistance genes to check whether the gene editing is successful or not with PCR.  We believe that the gene editing on Komagataeibacter xylinus DSM 6513 can bring a better future to solve global issues and bring a better planet for future humans.
