Why safety is important

The lab experiments are not always as safe as you might think. Most experiments involve some chemicals that are toxic or explosive. Even the smallest mistake could cause serious damage to our bodies. Thus, learning the safety rules is crucial before participating in any lab experiments.

General rules inside the laboratory

1. Always remember to have proper protection before entering any lab (lab suit, mask, shoe cover, gloves, goggles, etc.)
2. Use glass or plastic containers for anything that is strongly acidic or basic.
3. Always use a special heat container while mixing exothermic chemicals.
4. Follow the professor’s instructions when mixing chemicals.
5. Use the fume hood while dealing with toxic, corrosive, and explosive chemicals that are volatile.
6. When diluting acid, always drip acid into water.

7. Store acids and bases separately.
8. When dealing with volatile and highly flammable chemicals, use the fume hood and ensure there are no electric devices or anything that could cause sparks or flames.
9. Ensure your professor is always beside you during lab work.
10. Wash your hands completely before exiting the laboratory.
11. If you accidentally spill chemicals on your hand, wash with water instantly and inform your professor.
12. Remember to wash and clean containers after use.

Lab Practice

Before the formal lab starts, we go through 4 days of lab practice. We learned how to use some equipment inside the laboratory, including:
**How to use pipette:**
1. Check the maximum and minimum units of the pipette before using.
2. Always switch and dispose of the pipette after use.
3. The red number on the bottom scale wheel represents 0.1 μL, and the upper one represents 1000 μL.
This number represents 0.1 μL (marked in red circle).

How to use scale:

1. Always set the scale to 0 before measuring, especially when using paper or other containers for measuring.
2. If the weight is getting close to the desired number, start adding the chemical slowly by shaking your hand.
3. Never put extra chemical back into its container (I see too many students doing this).

pH Test Device:

1. Always clean the sensor with DD water after use.
2. Ensure the sensor is submerged in the solution before pressing the button for the most accurate data.

DO NOT do the following things in the laboratory!!!

1. Do not enter any room in the laboratory without permission.
2. Food and drink (including water) are never allowed in any lab.
3. Do not use open flames to heat the flask; use the heat plate instead.
4. Do not pour any chemicals or contaminated trash into the sink; use the chemical waste container instead.
5. Never put any chemical back into its container after use or handling; it may contaminate the rest of the chemicals inside.
6. Do not bring any lab materials or products outside the laboratory without permission.
7. Never try to repair any device inside the laboratory; if a device is broken, inform your professor.
8. Do not touch any electric devices with wet hands.
