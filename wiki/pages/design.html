Overview

Our project goal is to design a High-bacterial Cellulose production Bacteria and use it in multiple aspect. For example, to help solve the desertification problem or help to recover open wounds. We used the DNA PGI from the E. coli (allows bacteria to produce more bacterial cellulose) and applied it on Komagataeibacter xylinus DMS-6514, a naturally high-bacterial cellulose producer bacterium. After, we knocked out the GDH gene (the gene that produce glucose dehydrogenase). At last, we lest on the best environment setting of culturing the Komagataeibacter xylinus DMS-6514.

1. Why choose GDH gene

Bacterial cellulose can be produced from two substrates, glucose and fructose. GDH gene is the gene of membrane-bound glucose dehydrogenase. Glucose dehydrogenase is responsible for catalyzing the process of converting glucose to gluconic acid, using glucose as substrate, which means there will be less glucose for bacterial cellulose production. Thus, we decide to knockout the gene of GDH, and reduce the amount of gluconic acid production, so there will be more bacterial cellulose produced.

2. Why choose PGI gene

In the biosynthetic pathway of bacterial cellulose, the the presence of glucose-6-phosphate isomerase(PGI), fructose-6-phosphate can be catalyzed to produce glucose-6-phosphate, which is promising to enhance the metabolic flow of bacterial cellulose production. Hence, overexpessing the PGI gene can promote the bacterial cellulose production.

3. Why choose Komagataeibacter xylinus DSM 6513

Komagataeibacter xylinus DSM 6513 belongs to the groups of strains that are natural producers of bacterial cellulose, and has higher yields of bacterial cellulose production than other strains. Therefore, after the process of gene editing, bacterial cellulose can be produced more than before in K.xylinus, and the yields can increase significantly.

For higher production of bacterial cellulose, we design a few methods

1. Edition to the Gene of Komagataeibacter xylinus

Komagataeibacter xylinus is naturally high productive bacteria for producing bacterial cellulose, it have plenty amount of cellulase gene and produce plenty amount of cellulase for producing cellulose. However, the problem is that the k.xylinus have a GDH gene, which produce GDH (Glucose dehydrogenase). This enzyme can make glucose to glucose acid——a material that research currently found that it seems have no big effect on bacteria. Thus, we knocked out the GDH gene of k. xylinus strains by Homologous Recombination to make sure less glucose are used except from production of cellulose and life sustaining. More glucose then can be use in bacterial cellulose production. PGI gene(presence of glucose-6-phosphate isomerase) is a common found gene in bacteria. In the biosynthetic pathway of bacterial cellulose, in the presence of glucose-6-phosphate isomerase (PGI), fructose 6-phosphate can be catalysed to produce glucose 6-phosphate, which is promising to enhance the metabolic flow of BC production.

From this metabolic diagram, pgi has the function of catalyzing the conversion of fructose 6-p to glucose 6-p, which is the substrate for the synthesis of bacterial cellulose, so for this reason, the more pgi activity and amount are present, the more bacterial cellulose will be produce. According the research of KAIST on pgi and k.xylinus, the pgi produce by E.coli pgi gene have a relatively high specific activity compare to many other microorganisms. This mean the E.coli pgi gene is specially better in the way that we want: produce more substrate of BC production. We insert the pgi gene from E.coli into k.xylinus by plasmids.

2. Optimize the shake incubator speed and nutrient medium component

Komagataeibacter xylinus have a high production on bacterial cellulose, but it have a relatively low rate of reproduction during cultivating, thus we have to design to find a best environment both for its BC production and reproduction. In the study: Effect of Culture Conditions on Cellulose Production by a Komagataeibacter xylinus Strain, it mentioned that the spontaneous mutation of Komagataeibacter xylinus from Cel+ strain to Cel- strain could severely decrease the production of bacterial cellulose. The main reason of this mutation is proved to be the shear force of cultivating environment——under shake cultivation, the addition of xanthan gum will decrease the possibility for the K.xylinus to mutate to Cel- strain, thus increase the production of the BC. For know the exact environment for peak value of BC production, we designed the experiment and set 3 measures of the concentration of xanthan gum (0, 0.02%, 0.03%, 0.05%) and 3 different speed of shake incubator (50rmp，125rmp，200rmp), and map the growing rate curve graph base on the result.
